Product Requirements

PLEXOS Cloud is a Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) platform that enables energy market participants, utilities, system operators, planners, and analysts to model, simulate, optimize, and analyze power systems through a cloud-based environment. The platform provides scalable computation, collaboration capabilities, centralized data management, scenario analysis, and visualization tools to support energy market and transmission planning decisions.

Problem Statement

Energy companies currently face several challenges:

  • Complex desktop-based modeling workflows
  • Limited collaboration across teams
  • Long simulation execution times
  • Data version management issues
  • Difficulty sharing results with stakeholders
  • High infrastructure and maintenance costs

A cloud-native platform is required to streamline modeling workflows, improve collaboration, and accelerate decision-making.

Vision

Enable energy organizations to perform power system modeling, simulation, and planning from anywhere through a scalable, secure, and collaborative cloud platform.

Energy Market (Day Ahead)

Independent Power Producers

IPPs Objective: “How do I maximize revenue from my assets?”

Generators Produce Electricity

A gas-fired power plant offers 100 MWh of electricity in the Day-Ahead Market at $50/MWh. The ISO accepts this offer because it matches forecasted demand.

The generator is scheduled to run 100 MWh the next day.

Key point: The generator doesn’t sell directly to customers — it sells into the ISO-run market.

Why They Use PLEXOS:

To model their generation portfolios, optimize dispatch, and develop bidding strategies for day-ahead and real-time markets.

To evaluate fuel switching, renewables integration, and storage economics.

Examples: Shell, RWE Supply & Trading, SN Aboitiz Power, Mabuhay Energy Corporation.

Independent System Operator

ISO Objective: “How do I balance the system fairly?”

Market Operators (middleman — doesn’t own or profit from energy)

The ISO clears the market: it matches supply (generators) and demand (utilities / LSEs).

Market-clearing price = $50/MWh.
Generator is committed to produce 100 MWh.
Utility is committed to buy 100 MWh.

Key point: The ISO does not keep the money or electricity. It acts as a clearinghouse, ensuring financial settlement between sellers and buyers.

Load Serving Entities

Utility Objective: “How do I buy power cheaply?”

Utilities (Actual buyer of wholesale energy and seller to retail customers)

The utility (say, City Power Co.) has 100 MWh of demand forecasted for its customers. It bids in the DAM and is awarded 100 MWh at $50/MWh.

Utility cost = 100 × $50 = $5,000 (paid to the market/ISO).

Key point: Utilities use the ISO market to procure energy on behalf of their retail customers.

Why They Use PLEXOS:

For demand forecasting, procurement planning, and resource adequacy assessments.

To evaluate long-term investments (e.g., new solar, wind, gas, or storage).

Examples: Alliant Energy, San Diego Gas & Electric, Energy Australia, Territory Generation.

Customer / Indirect Users (pays for energy, wires, and reliability costs)

End-use customers (homes, factories, offices) consume the 100 MWh.

Why They Use PLEXOS:

Energy Market (Real-Time)

Available generators in the Real-Time Market at, say, $120/MWh The ISO procures extra 10 MWh from other available generators in the Real-Time Market at, say, $120/MWh.

The utility must pay: 10 Ã— $120 = $1,200 extra. Total utility cost = $5,000 (DAM) + $1,200 (RTM) = $6,200.

If customer demand really was 110 MWh, their bill increases accordingly (e.g., 110 Ã— $70 = $7,700).

Pendo analysis of PLEXOS Cloud provided valuable insights into user behavior, feature adoption, and navigation patterns across key workflows. Ultimately, Pendo enabled a more data-driven approach to enhancing usability, increasing feature adoption, and improving overall user satisfaction.
